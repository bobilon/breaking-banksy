Article 7 — The Two Websites
## Banksy Explained, Banksy-Value, and the Infrastructure Built Around a Bubble

In 2008, two websites appeared that deserve examination as a pair.

Banksyexplained.com and banksy-value.com are not, in themselves, remarkable things. Explanatory websites and price-tracking websites exist for many artists. What is remarkable is their timing and their complementarity — and the corporate record that surrounds their launch.

An explanation infrastructure tells a buyer what a work means. A valuation infrastructure tells a buyer what a work is worth. You build both of these simultaneously when you are trying to convert a street art phenomenon into a stable investment-grade asset class. The explanation website provides the cultural justification. The valuation website provides the financial floor. Together they perform the function that a prospectus performs in a securities offering: they give the buyer a reason to believe the price is real. The two sites launched together. The public record suggests they were managed by the same webmaster. That claim is stated here as inference pending full confirmation of the underlying registration data, but the circumstantial case for it is strong enough to warrant the inference.

---

Banksy Explained's named author and apparent editorial controlling hand is Sébastien Laboureau. French-born. Écoles des Mines de Paris engineer. MSc Finance from the London School of Economics. Former Goldman Sachs mergers and acquisitions banker. Independent art advisor based in Miami. Certified IFAA appraiser. His byline appears on virtually every post on the site. The About page names him as the primary author and describes the site as the work of "a group of art collectors and art enthusiasts who have been following Banksy for over fifteen years," but Laboureau is the only named individual who writes content.

His other ventures are directly relevant. Moonstar Fine Arts Advisors LLC is his advisory vehicle. IntelArt.io is an art market intelligence platform he built. A prior role as Resident Art Advisor at the Sagamore Hotel Miami Beach saw him curating street art shows including works by Banksy, Fairey, D\*Face, Inkie, and others. He describes himself as a recognised Banksy expert with close relationships with auction houses and private collectors in New York, London, Paris, and Zurich.

Moonstar Fine Arts Advisors LLC was incorporated in Florida on 25 April 2008. The date places it at the beginning of the primary bubble's institutional phase — the same year the two websites launch, the same year the Banksy market begins its long acceleration toward the £111 million Beautiful Inside My Head Forever Sotheby's event in September 2008. Moonstar's public profile through the bubble years was minimal. It was not a functioning advisory business with a visible client roster. It became named and visible at the point when attribution questions about the Banksy enterprise intensified and when buyers and their legal representatives needed something to point to as an institutional validator.

Moonstar Fine Arts Advisors LLC was administratively dissolved on 26 September 2025 — as the legal environment around the enterprise became materially more complex, five months before the Ant and Dec High Court proceedings became public record. The corporate lifecycle of Moonstar is the corporate lifecycle of a pass-through structure: incorporated early enough to claim institutional longevity, dormant publicly through the bubble years, activated as a named liability vehicle when attribution became necessary, dissolved as legal exposure increased.

---

The banksy-value.com database is the other half of the infrastructure.

The site operates as a comprehensive auction price tracker for the Banksy secondary market — lot data, hammer prices, edition records, provenance annotations. Its dataset was used as the primary source for the University of Liverpool peer-reviewed study of the Banksy print bubble published in SN Business & Economics in March 2024, authored by Dr Stephen Clark. The study's data availability statement notes that the datasets "are not publicly available due to being the property of the www.banksy-value.com web site owner" and directs researchers to contact that owner directly. The owner is not publicly identified on the site.

The Clark/Liverpool study analysed 2,884 lots, 2,577 after exclusions, and applied a Bayesian random effects negative binomial regression model to the price series. Its findings are worth stating in full because they are the academic confirmation of what the corporate record describes structurally.

Before the pandemic, the long-run average auction price for a Banksy print was approximately £10,000, growing at roughly 19.5% compounded annually from 2010 to 2019. Clark flags that even this pre-pandemic growth fitted an exponential model unusually well — the market was already running hot before the bubble proper began. The COVID-era surge, which Clark identifies as running from approximately mid-2020 through 2021, took average prices to multiples of that baseline. The study confirms the bubble as a statistically significant departure from trend rather than an acceleration of it. It also confirms that the deflation was real and sustained — prices fell back toward pre-bubble levels as the supply conditions that had driven the surge normalised.

What the study does not examine, because it is not the study's scope, is the supply-side mechanics of the bubble: who controlled the inventory that drove prices up, what the relationship was between the primary market publisher and the authentication body during the bubble years, and what the simultaneous launch of a comprehensive explanation infrastructure and a comprehensive valuation infrastructure in 2008 implies about the market's construction.

---

The supply-side mechanics are what the corporate record explains.

Pictures on Walls held the primary market for Banksy editions. Pest Control Office held the authentication function — the COA issuance that determined which works could trade on the secondary market with institutional confidence. For twelve years, from PCO's incorporation in January 2008 to its acquisition by POW in January 2020, the authentication body and the primary market publisher were nominally separate entities sharing directors and a network of operational relationships while maintaining the legal form of independence.

The independence was the market's premise. A COA issued by an entity that is legally independent of the publisher means something different than a COA issued by an entity that is wholly owned by the publisher. It means that the authentication is a third-party attestation rather than a manufacturer's mark. The Banksy secondary market's price floor was built on the premise that PCO's authentication was a third-party attestation. When POW acquired greater than 75% of PCO in January 2020, that premise ended. The authentication body became a quality control function for the publisher's own inventory. The certificates became manufacturer's marks. The market had been operating on a fiction for twelve years, and the vertical integration made the fiction visible to anyone who checked the Companies House filings.

The bubble peaked in 2021. The deflation follows the vertical integration by approximately one year — long enough for the market to absorb the structural change, short enough that the causal relationship between the integration and the price correction is visible in the data.

---

The two websites, read against the corporate record, are a market infrastructure project rather than a fan resource project.

Banksy Explained provides the taxonomy — the authenticated catalogue, the edition records, the originals designations, the print charts whose arithmetic encodes the enterprise's ownership structure in public HTML. As Articles 1 through 4 of this series established, the site was built by someone with granular knowledge of the enterprise's settlement terms. It functions as the authenticated ledger of the estate, maintained under the terms of what the post-2020 POW/PCO consolidated entity can and cannot revise without breaking its internal consistency.

Banksy-value.com provides the price discovery — the auction record that tells a buyer what the catalogue items are worth on the secondary market. Its data was proprietary enough that a peer-reviewed academic study had to obtain special access to it. Its owner is not publicly identified.

The two sites together constitute the informational infrastructure of a managed market: a catalogue that tells you what exists and certifies it, and a price database that tells you what it trades for. This is the infrastructure you build when you are converting a cultural phenomenon into an investment product. It is also the infrastructure you need if you are managing inventory across both the primary and secondary markets simultaneously — if the authenticated catalogue and the price discovery mechanism are both under the influence of the same parties who hold the supply.

Whether the webmaster linkage between the two sites is confirmed as a single individual or resolves into a shared operational relationship between the sites' respective owners, the structural point holds: the two pieces of market infrastructure that a rational secondary market operator would need were both built in the same year, in apparent coordination, around the same corporate event — the Moonstar incorporation that placed a named, credentialled, Florida-registered art advisory entity at the centre of the market's information architecture at the moment the bubble was beginning to form.

Moonstar dissolved in September 2025. The Ant and Dec proceedings followed in March 2026. The infrastructure that was built to support the bubble was wound down in the same sequence that the bubble's legal consequences arrived.

---

The sequence is the argument. The two websites launch in 2008. Moonstar incorporates in 2008. The bubble builds from 2010 to 2021. PCO is acquired by POW in January 2020. The bubble peaks in 2021 and deflates. Moonstar dissolves in September 2025. BBAY Art dissolves in January 2026. The High Court proceedings go public in March 2026.

Every element of the market's information and advisory infrastructure was built, operated, and wound down in a sequence that corresponds precisely to the market's construction and subsequent legal exposure. That is not coincidence. It is the record of a managed operation, legible in the public record for anyone willing to read the dates in order.
